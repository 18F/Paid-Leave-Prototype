How this works

This estimator calculates the benefits you will receive based on the amount of money you made during the year preceding your claim date (your “base period”). It assumes that you file your claim 18 days before your benefits begin (18 days is the current length of time it takes us to process claims). Finally, the estimator assumes a minimum wage of $12/hr ($24,000 per year).

If you earned You are eligible for
Less than $36,000 90% of your weekly wages
More than $36,000 $623 per week + 50% of your weekly wages

If you earned less than $36,000 (150% of the minimum wage) during your base period you are eligible to receive 90% of your weekly wages per week. If you earned more than $36,000 during your base period you are eligible to receive $623 per week (90% of 150% of minimum wage) plus 50% of your earnings. The maximum possible benefit is $1,000 per week.

Note: In some cases, you may substitute wages paid before your base period to make your claim valid or increase your benefit amount. Contact us if your base period was adversely affected by military service, industrial disability, trade dispute, or long-term unemployment. (An industrial disability is one caused by a job-related injury or illness. A trade dispute is a dispute between your employer and you related to the terms of your employment. Long-term unemployment is unemployment of 27 weeks or more.)
