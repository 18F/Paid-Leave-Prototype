How this works

This estimator calculates the benefits you will receive based on the amount of money you make during the year preceding your claim date. This year-long period is also known as your base period. The estimator assumes a minimum wage of $12/hr ($21,120 per year).

If you earned… You are eligible for
Less than $31,680 90% of your weekly wages
More than $31,680 $609 per week + 50% of your weekly wages

If you earned less than $31,680 (150% of the minimum wage) during your base period you are eligible to receive 90% of your weekly wages per week. If you earned more than $31,680 during your base period you are eligible to receive $609 per week (90% of 150% of minimum wage) plus 50% of your earnings. The maximum possible benefit is $1,000 per week.

Note: Under certain circumstances, you may substitute wages paid prior to your base period in order to make a claim valid and/or increase your weekly benefit amount. Contact us if your base period was adversely affected by military service, industrial disability, trade dispute, or long-term unemployment.
