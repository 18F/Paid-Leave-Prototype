How this works

This calculator estimates the benefits you will receive based on the amount of money you make during the year preceding your claim date. This year-long period is known as your base period.

You are eligible for 90% wage replacement if you earn less than 150% of the minimum wage during your base period. You are eligible to recceive 90% of 150% of minimum wage plus 50% of your earnings if you earn more than this. The maximum possible benefit is $1,000 per week. More information.

Note: Under certain circumstances, you may substitute wages paid prior to your base period in order to make a claim valid and/or increase your weekly benefit amount. Contact us if your base period was adversely affected by military service, industrial disability, trade dispute, or long-term unemployment.
