Healthcare providers

Healthcare providers play a critical role in the filing and processing of paid family medical leave benefits. Healthcare providers are required to submit supplemental information that will help the state evaluate a claim for benefits.

Providing supplemental information

Healthcare providers have two options for providing information about their patients. First, healthcare providers can print or request a copy of the supplemental information form.

Second, healthcare providers can provide supplemental information through this website itself. All claimants are given a verification code to pass along to their healthcare provider after filing a claim for benefits online. Healthcare providers can use this code to sign in and provide supplemental information.

Our review process

[State name] takes the following factors into account when reviewing the information submitted by healthcare providers :

  • The quality, accuracy, and timeliness of medical certifications.
  • The diagnosis, International Classification of Diseases (ICD) codes, and estimated recovery dates.

[State name] cross-references the information submitted by the person filing the claim with the information submitted by healthcare providers. When an estimated recovery date for a specific diagnosis is substantially more we would normally expect, [State name] will reach out to the implicated parties for additional information. In special circumstances, we may send the person filing for benefits to an independent medical examiner for a second opinion regarding their ability to perform their regular and customary work.

Injury

If a patient has an industrial related illness or injury, healthcare providers are encouraged to have the patient contact his/her employer regarding workers’ compensation benefits.
